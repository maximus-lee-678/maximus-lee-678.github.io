Hobbies

🎶 In my free time, I like to build up and listen to my personal music catalogue. Being a fan of an older time period, I enjoy rock, R&B, pop and synth-pop. I also enjoy listening to Video Game Music (VGM) when studying or working. Some games have really good scores, for example Faster Than Light, Starbound or Hearthstone.

🎶 In addition to the aforementioned songs of the 80s, I'm also a big fan of the synths behind some of them. It's a whole genre of its own: Retro-inspired electronic; including, but not limited to, synthwave, retrowave, darksynth and synthpop. It all began back in Polytechnic, when I stumbled across Kung Fury, an 80s parody by LaserUnicorns. I was hooked by the score, drawn to its vibrance & flashiness. Ever since, this genre has been my one-size fits all for any situation.

🎮 I also enjoy playing video games. Doesn't mean im good at them though... Nowadays, I can only really find the time to play Team Fortress 2, but there are many other games I recommend. Games such as:

  • 🏯 Sandbox - Minecraft

  • 👨‍🌾 Farming Sim - Stardew Valley
  • 🏹 Action Adventure - Terraria
  • ✨ Looter Shooter - Borderlands 2

🎮 Give 'em a try if you feel like it!

Software

I'm terrible at art, be it visual or musical. However, I've always wanted to create something I can call my own. I was introduced to my first programming language (C++ in Polytechnic), and the rest is history.

My toolbelt consists of C, C++, C#, Java, Kotlin, Python, PHP and JavaScript. Some of my projects and utilities, either solo or cooperative, can be found in the "Projects" section. A pattern you may observe is most (ok, all) of my personal utility programs are written in C. Although I may not be the best at it, I enjoy what it has to offer, namely the pointers and "low" level memory management. Beware the triple pointer!

There was even that one time I wrote a web scraper using C and the CURL library. Many argue C's not the right tool for the job, and I would be better off using something like Python, and I agree. But where's the fun in that?

About

In the earlier days of the internet, there existed many static webpages created by many individuals, the likes of which can still be found on Neocities, for example. Some have really cozy vibes, others ooze aesthetic, and a few show off their technical prowess. Seeing the creativity and expression of others inspired me to create my own little corner on the internet.

Much like my music tastes, I also love the 80s retro aesthetic. The website needs to have neon lights, gridlines, setting sun, the whole lot!

This website was written using HTML, CSS and JavaScript with jQuery. Mobile view's still in its infancy, stay tuned!
